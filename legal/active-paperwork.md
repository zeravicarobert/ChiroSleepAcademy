## Subcategory 3 — Veteran question from Consumer Affairs / Chiro Board

- Both boards are asking if Robert is a veteran (via a paper to
  reply)
- Robert's questions: *"Should I fill this out? Why is the Consumer
  Affairs Board asking this, and why is my Chiro Board asking it?"*

### Answer: YES — fill it out. Mark veteran.

**Why both boards ask:**

- The **California Department of Consumer Affairs** oversees ~40
  professional licensing boards (including the Chiropractic Board).
  They share intake infrastructure — so the same vet-status question
  appears on multiple boards' forms.
- California offers **vet-specific benefits across licensing**:
  - Initial license **fee waivers** for veterans (Business and
    Professions Code §114.5)
  - Expedited license processing for veterans and military spouses
  - Counting of military experience toward licensing requirements
    in some professions
- Marking VET on these forms can unlock fee waivers and expedited
  treatment without any downside.

### What Robert should do

- **Fill out as YES, veteran** (USMC, 9 years, 4th Force Recon, 3D
  ANGLICO per CLAUDE.md §19)
- Return to both Boards
- Ask each Board: *"As a veteran, what benefits am I eligible for —
  fee waivers, expedited renewal, etc.?"*

### Side note worth knowing

The vet question on these forms is **also a flag for tracking**
the percentage of CA professionals who are veterans (state demographics
research). So filling it out also helps the state understand the
chiropractic workforce. Pure upside.

### What LT40 actually is

IRS notice **LT40** is a **balance-due / outstanding-tax notice**,
typically part of the IRS collection workflow. Depending on the
specific letter, it may be:

- A **payment reminder** (early collection stage)
- A **notice of intent to collect** via federal payment levy
- A precursor to **CP90 / LT11 / Letter 1058**, which are the formal
  *"Final Notice of Intent to Levy"* letters that start the 30-day
  Collection Due Process appeal clock

**Robert needs to determine which specific LT40 variant this is.**
Different LT40 letters have different urgency and different response
options.

### ⚠️ Important time-sensitivity

If the LT40 carries a 30-day response window, that window was
**4/14/26 → 5/14/26** — which **has already passed**. This means:

- The standard appeal window may be closed
- IRS could be approaching active collection (levy on bank accounts,
  garnishment of payments)
- Standard non-appeal options remain: full payment, installment
  agreement, Offer in Compromise, Currently Not Collectible status
- **Robert should NOT handle this alone**

### What Robert MUST do

**This week — engage a tax professional.** Options:

1. **Enrolled Agent (EA)** — licensed by IRS, can represent Robert
   in collection matters. Lower fees than tax attorney. Find via
   **naea.org** (National Association of Enrolled Agents).
2. **Tax attorney** — required if litigation or criminal exposure
   is in play (unlikely at this stage, but possible given the
   ~$48K and ~13-year-old liability). State Bar of California
   referral.
3. **Tax resolution firm** — be cautious; this space has scammy
   operators. Look for: licensed CPA or EA on staff, transparent
   fees, no "guaranteed pennies on the dollar" promises.

### What Robert should NOT do

- ❌ Call the 800 number cold without representation (anything

- ❌ Pay $47K without first confirming the assessment is accurate

  (installment, OIC, hardship, statute of limitations on collection

  2013-year liability may be approaching or past the statute,
