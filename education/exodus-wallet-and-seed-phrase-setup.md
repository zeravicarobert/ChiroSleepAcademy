# Exodus Wallet Setup — Step by Step (5th-grade English)

**Purpose:** Plain-language step-by-step for setting up an Exodus
Bitcoin wallet and recording the seed phrase safely.

**Audience:** Robert. Written at 5th-grade reading level per his
request 5/28/26.

**Context:** Eleanor (Robert's Bitcoin course teacher) asked him to
get an Exodus wallet during the Day 2 class.

**Why this matters:** Your seed phrase is the single most important
thing in your whole Bitcoin life. Anyone with those 12 words has
all your Bitcoin. Get this part right.

---

# Part 1 — Get the Exodus wallet

## Step 1. Go to the right website

Open your web browser. Type this exactly:

```
exodus.com
```

⚠️ **Be careful.** Bad people make fake websites with names that
look almost the same (like `exodus-wallet.com` or `exodus.io`).
Only the real `exodus.com` is safe.

## Step 2. Click Download

On the Exodus website, find the big **"Download"** button. Click it.

## Step 3. Pick your device

- **Mac** — if you use an Apple computer
- **Windows** — if you use a PC
- **iPhone** — if you have an Apple phone
- **Android** — if you have a Samsung, Google, or other phone

You can install Exodus on more than one device later.

## Step 4. Install the app

- On a **phone**, the website will send you to the App Store or
  Google Play. Tap "Get" or "Install."
- On a **computer**, a file will download. Click the file and
  follow the steps to install.

## Step 5. Open Exodus

Find the Exodus icon. Tap or click it.

## Step 6. Click "Create New Wallet"

Pick **Create New Wallet** (not "Restore Wallet").

## Step 7. Set a strong password

This password unlocks the app on this device.

A good password:
- At least 12 letters, numbers, and symbols
- NOT a word you've used before
- NOT your birthday or name

**Write the password down on paper.** Keep it in a different place
from your seed phrase.

## Step 8. STOP. Do Part 2 right away.

Don't put it off. Don't add Bitcoin yet. Record your seed phrase
first.

---

# Part 2 — Record your seed phrase

## What a seed phrase is

12 words that Exodus picks for you. These 12 words can restore
your whole wallet on any device.

**Anyone with these 12 words has all your Bitcoin.**

## Step 1. Find the seed phrase in Exodus

1. Tap or click **Settings** (gear icon ⚙️)
2. Tap or click **Backup**
3. Type your password
4. Exodus shows the **12 words**

## Step 2. Get ready to write

Before the words appear:

- ✏️ Pen (not pencil)
- 📄 **Two pieces of paper**
- Quiet place, no one watching
- No cameras pointed at you (turn off baby monitors, home security
  cameras in the room)
- Phone face-down

## Step 3. Write each word in order

Order matters. Write them in the exact order Exodus shows.

```
1. ________________
2. ________________
3. ________________
4. ________________
5. ________________
6. ________________
7. ________________
8. ________________
9. ________________
10. _______________
11. _______________
12. _______________
```

## Step 4. Check your work TWICE

Spelling matters. `aware` and `away` are different words.

If even one letter is wrong, the words won't work later.

## Step 5. Make a second copy

Same 12 words. Same order. On a second piece of paper.

## Step 6. Hide the papers in TWO different places

**Don't put both papers in the same place.** If one fire destroys
one, the other survives.

Good places:
- Fireproof safe at home (best)
- Bank safety deposit box (very good)
- Hidden spot in a different room of your house
- Trusted family member's safe

Bad places:
- ❌ Wallet you carry
- ❌ Car
- ❌ Desk drawer in plain sight
- ❌ Anywhere a guest could open

## Step 7. Tell NO ONE the words

- ❌ Don't tell your spouse (unless you've decided together)
- ❌ Don't tell your kids
- ❌ Don't say them near smart speakers (Alexa, Siri, Google Home)
- ❌ Don't read them on a phone call
- ❌ Don't text them

## Step 8. NEVER do these — ever

- ❌ Photo of the seed phrase
- ❌ Type it into ANY computer (not Notes, Word, Google Docs, Excel,
  email, password manager — anything)
- ❌ Save to cloud (iCloud, Google Drive, Dropbox)
- ❌ Email to yourself
- ❌ Tell a customer service person
- ❌ Type into a website that asks for it

**Real Exodus support will NEVER ask for your seed phrase.** If
anyone asks, the answer is no.

## Step 9. Confirm it worked

Exodus may ask you to prove you wrote it down by picking some
specific words ("What's word #7?").

Look at your paper. Pick the right words. Done.

# What you have now

- ✅ Exodus app installed
- ✅ A wallet you control
- ✅ Two paper copies of your seed phrase, in two safe places

You're now your own bank.

---

# Next steps (when you're ready)

When you want to add Bitcoin to your wallet:

- Buy a small amount inside Exodus (they have a buy feature)
- Send Bitcoin from an exchange like Coinbase to your Exodus address
- Receive Bitcoin from someone else

Take a breath before you do that. Make sure your seed phrase paper
copies are rock solid first.

---

# If something goes wrong

| Problem | What to do |
|---|---|
| I forgot my password | Use your seed phrase to restore the wallet. Then set a new password. |
| I lost my paper seed phrase but still have my device | Open Exodus → Settings → Backup → write it down again. Do this NOW. |
| I lost my paper AND my device | Your Bitcoin is gone. There is no customer service that can recover it. (This is why two paper copies in different places matters.) |
| Someone might have seen my seed phrase | Create a new wallet with a new seed phrase. Send any Bitcoin from the old wallet to the new one as fast as you can. The old wallet is no longer safe. |

---

# A note about the future

This is a software wallet (a "hot wallet"). It's good for learning
and small amounts. When you hold more Bitcoin in the future, look at
a **hardware wallet** like Ledger or Trezor (see CLAUDE.md §19 —
Robert plans to buy one in the future).

A hardware wallet stores the seed phrase on a device that never
connects to the internet. Even safer.

For now, Exodus + a well-protected paper seed phrase is the right
starting point.
