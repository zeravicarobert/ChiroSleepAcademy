## 2. What this program is NOT *(§5 non-negotiables, stated up front)*

CSA's standing ethics require these to be on the table from the
beginning — not in fine print:

- **This is NOT a cure for sleep apnea.** UAOSA is a serious medical
  condition. This program is a *structural-medicine treatment option*
  that addresses biomechanical alignment — a layer that is typically
  missing from standard sleep apnea care. Existing treatments (CPAP,
  oral appliances, surgery, pharmacology) remain appropriate within
  their lanes; this program is built on the claim that they are
  *insufficient alone*, not that they are wrong.
- **This does NOT replace CPAP if your sleep physician has
  prescribed it.** The program works alongside standard sleep
  medicine, not in opposition to it.
- **This is NOT a guarantee of any specific outcome.** AHI/ODI
  improvements vary by individual. We measure and document; we don't
  promise.
- **Outside-of-scope care is NOT included** — surgery, hospitalization,
  emergency care, oncology, medications, and conditions outside the
  structural-airway scope are referred out to appropriate providers.

  hardship-inducing — we'd rather refer them to lower-cost options

positioned for a specific echelon, because the program's structure
(multi-provider, year-long, travel-required) does not scale to lower
price points without compromising the work.

At $25K, CSA's program sits **below the surgical and implant
alternatives** while offering a structural, coordinated, non-surgical
pathway that addresses the biomechanical layer none of the others
do. For the right patient — the structurally-aware, CBP-engaged,
professional-class UAOSA patient — this is a competitive price for
the work.

### The "certain echelon" defined

Most likely fits:

  business owners, senior tech, surgeons, dentists)
