# CSA Sleep & Airway Screening Questionnaire

**Version 1.0 — Draft (5/27/26)**

**Purpose:** Identify patients who may benefit from a Chiropractic Sleep
Academy (CSA) Structural UAOSA Recovery Program 4-Way Consultation.

**Administered by:** Chiropractors (especially CBP-certified DCs in Dr.
Deed Harrison's network), dentists, or other licensed clinicians
trained in CSA's referral pathway. Self-administration permitted for
Parts A, B, and D; Part C requires clinician observation.

**Time required:** ~10 minutes.

---

## ⚠️ Important — read before administering

This is a **SCREENING tool, NOT a diagnostic instrument.**

Only a sleep physician using **polysomnography (PSG)** or **home sleep
testing (HST)** can diagnose obstructive sleep apnea (OSA), upper
airway obstructive sleep apnea (UAOSA), central sleep apnea, or
related disorders.

This questionnaire identifies **structural, biomechanical, and
sleep-related indicators** that may warrant a coordinated CSA
evaluation and a sleep physician referral.

**Pre-deployment legal/clinical review required.** This draft should
be reviewed by a sleep physician and a healthcare attorney before
being used with real patients.

## Part B — Epworth Sleepiness Scale (Daytime Sleepiness)

> *Source: Johns, 1991. Validated measure of daytime sleepiness.*

How likely are you to doze or fall asleep in the following
situations, in contrast to feeling just tired? Use this scale:

- **0** = would *never* doze
- **1** = *slight* chance of dozing
- **2** = *moderate* chance of dozing
- **3** = *high* chance of dozing

## Part C — CSA Structural-Airway Screen *(Original to CSA)*

> *Source: Chiropractic Sleep Academy, 2026. The biomechanical layer
> typically missing from standard sleep apnea screening.*

This is the head-to-toe structural layer CSA is built around:
**cranium → spine → pelvis → feet.** Many of these items require
clinician observation.

### Cranio-facial / Oral (Y/N)

| # | Item | Y/N | Self/Clin |
|---|---|---|---|
| C1 | Do you breathe primarily through your mouth at night? | | Self |
| C2 | Do you wake with a dry mouth? | | Self |
| C3 | Has anyone told you that you grind your teeth at night (bruxism)? | | Self |
| C4 | Do you experience jaw pain, TMJ clicking, or jaw locking? | | Self |
| C5 | Is the tongue scalloped along the lateral edges? | | **Clin** |
| C6 | Have you had any teeth extracted for orthodontic purposes? | | Self |
| C7 | Have you had braces or other orthodontic work? | | Self |
| C8 | Mallampati Class III or IV on visual exam? | | **Clin** |

> *Mallampati visual scoring:*
> - **I** — Soft palate, uvula, and tonsils fully visible
> - **II** — Soft palate and uvula visible; tonsils partially hidden
> - **III** — Soft and hard palate visible; base of uvula visible
> - **IV** — Only hard palate visible
> *Classes III and IV correlate with elevated OSA risk.*

## Stratification & Recommendation

### Combine all three parts

| Combination | Stratification | Recommended Next Step |
|---|---|---|
| STOP-BANG ≥ 5 **AND/OR** Epworth ≥ 16 **AND/OR** CSA Structural ≥ 10 | 🔴 **RED — High UAOSA Risk** | **Refer immediately to sleep physician for evaluation. Concurrently schedule 4-Way CSA Consultation.** |
| STOP-BANG 3–4 **OR** Epworth 11–15 **OR** CSA Structural 5–9 (none in red range) | 🟡 **YELLOW — Moderate Risk / Strong CSA Candidate** | Sleep medicine evaluation recommended; **schedule 4-Way CSA Consultation for structural workup.** |
| All three parts in lowest band | 🟢 **GREEN — Low Risk** | Continue current care. **Re-screen in 12 months** or sooner if symptoms change. Address any structural concerns in routine chiropractic care. |
| Patient already diagnosed with OSA/UAOSA (Part D #2 = Yes) | **REGARDLESS OF SCORES** | **Schedule 4-Way CSA Consultation.** Patient already qualifies for the UAOSA pathway. |

## Important Disclosures & Disclaimers

**Not a diagnostic instrument.** This questionnaire does not
diagnose sleep apnea, UAOSA, central sleep apnea, or any related
disorder. Only a sleep physician using polysomnography (PSG) or
home sleep testing (HST) can make a diagnosis.

**Not a replacement for medical care.** This screening does not
replace medical advice from a sleep physician, primary care
physician, or other licensed clinician. If you have current concerns
about your breathing during sleep, daytime sleepiness, or sleep-
related cardiovascular risk, consult your sleep physician
immediately.

**Existing care continues.** If you are currently using CPAP, an
oral appliance, or any other prescribed sleep apnea treatment, do
NOT discontinue without consulting your prescribing physician. The
CSA program is offered alongside standard sleep medicine care, not
in opposition to it.

**Validated instruments.** STOP-BANG (Chung et al., 2008) and the
Epworth Sleepiness Scale (Johns, 1991) are validated screening
instruments used by sleep medicine clinicians worldwide. The CSA
Structural-Airway Screen (Part C) is original to Chiropractic
Sleep Academy and reflects the biomechanical assessment framework
of Structural Airway Chiropractic; it is not yet independently
validated.

**Privacy.** This screening collects protected health information
(PHI). All responses are kept confidential per HIPAA and applicable
state law. Information will only be shared with the CSA care team
with your explicit consent.

## For CSA internal use (not on patient-facing version)

### Deployment dependencies

Before this questionnaire is given to a real patient:

1. **Sleep physician review.** Have a board-certified sleep
   physician review for clinical accuracy. Especially the
   stratification logic and the recommended-next-step language.
2. **Healthcare attorney review.** Have a healthcare attorney review
   for compliance — HIPAA, scope-of-practice across states,
   liability disclaimers, advertising rules. Especially relevant if
   the form will be used outside California.
3. **CSA-specific Part C validation.** Part C (the structural
   screen) is original. Over time, CSA should collect data showing
   that high Part C scores correlate with HST findings — this is
   how the CSA Structural Screen becomes a validated instrument.
4. **Digital form production.** Convert this Markdown draft into:
   - A fillable PDF (for paper / email administration)
   - A digital web form (Typeform, JotForm, or ClickFunnels 2.0)
   - Patient version (Parts A, B, D, E1) + Clinician version (adds
     Part C visual exam items + scoring)
5. **Branding pass.** Add CSA logo, branding, footer with CSA
   contact information.
6. **Translation.** If serving non-English-speaking patients,
   translation may be required (especially Spanish for CA market).

### Scoring tool

A simple spreadsheet (Excel/Google Sheets) or web calculator that
auto-scores all three sub-totals and outputs the stratification
recommendation. Reduces human scoring errors.

### Integration with EHR

When ChiroTouch onboarding is complete (§19 software stack),
consider importing this screening as a structured intake form so
results live in the patient's EHR record.

### Data tracking

Track aggregated screening outcomes over time:
- % of screenings that produce Red / Yellow / Green
- % of Yellow/Red screenings that convert to 4-Way Consultations
- % of 4-Way Consultations that convert to Program enrollments
- HST/PSG results in screened patients (correlation with Part C)

This data validates the funnel and the Part C instrument over time.
